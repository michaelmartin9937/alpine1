Frequently Asked Questions

What is dry ice blasting?
Dry ice blasting is a form of non-abrasive cleaning that uses recycled CO2 pellets as the impact medium. These dry ice particles are blasted at supersonic speeds, lifting and removing contaminants from the work surface.
How does it work?
Unlike other blasting methods which primarily rely on the kinetic force to remove contaminants, dry ice incorporates other factors that result in a much more efficient and less damaging outcome. To simplify, the process works in three main factors; kinetic energy, thermal shock, and a combined thermal-kinetic effect.

The initial kinetic effect is caused by a high speed collision between the dry ice particles and the cleaning surface. Because of the properties of solid carbon dioxide, it instantaneously evaporates upon impact.

As a result of the below freezing temperatures, a thermal shear effect is created. This effect happens from the extreme and sudden temperature change on the surface of the cleaning area. It then causes the contaminants to become brittle and break its bond from the surface.

Finally, the dry ice pellets sublimate and cause a rapid expansion upon impact. This effect efficiently removes the contaminant without any secondary waste or residue.
Will it damage any components on my vehicle?
This process will not damage any components when applied properly, including sensitive electronics. Because it’s non-conductive and leaves no residue there is no “dry out” period needed. Great care is taken when it comes to old and brittle plastics to avoid any damage.
Can I bring engines, transmissions, or parts to be sprayed?
Absolutely, call ahead and we will provide a custom quote based on the job provided. Majority of jobs can be done the same day.
How effective is dry ice cleaning?
Dry Ice cleaning is extremely efficient. With vehicles and restoration projects, it can clean and restore in less than half the time and effort as compared to more traditional methods. Most importantly, it cleans without the use of corrosive and harmful solvents. Another attractive attribute is that the cleaning process is minimally invasive, meaning that far fewer parts and hardware need to be removed in order to reach contaminated areas.
Is dry ice cleaning environmentally friendly?
It is very environmentally friendly, it does not create any secondary waste or debris. The CO2 needed to create the dry ice is pulled from the atmosphere, therefore not creating any in the process.
What can dry ice blasting remove?
Dry Ice blasting can remove almost any surface contaminants, such as oil, petroleum products, light rust, organic debris, and adhesives.
Does dry ice blasting remove rust?
Yes, it will remove light and loosely bonded surface rust, but heavily rusted and oxidized areas would need to be removed with more traditional methods such as sanding and grinding. Areas we’ve treated for rust will need to be protected to prevent any future corrosion from occurring.
